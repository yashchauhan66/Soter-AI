# Public Launch Checklist

Do not launch from a calendar date alone. Launch when the evidence and operational gates below are satisfied.

## Product and Reliability

- [ ] Landing page reviewed on desktop and mobile.
- [ ] Pricing page matches the offers currently being tested.
- [ ] Demo page works with synthetic data.
- [ ] Documentation and SDK examples work.
- [ ] Trust center, privacy, terms, responsible disclosure, and subprocessors pages reviewed.
- [ ] Status page and incident workflow operational.
- [ ] Signup, first project, first API key, first request, and first report tested.
- [ ] Billing, cancellation, and support paths tested.

## Conversion Paths

- [ ] Free audit CTA identifies owned or authorized systems.
- [ ] Agency partner CTA routes to a monitored inbox or form.
- [ ] Contact sales and enterprise pilot CTAs work.
- [ ] Demo booking process has available time slots and an owner.
- [ ] Follow-up expectations are visible.

## Evidence

- [ ] One permissioned case study or explicitly approved anonymized beta result.
- [ ] No invented customer, logo, testimonial, benchmark, ROI, or revenue claim.
- [ ] All metrics include period, denominator, and source.
- [ ] OWASP alignment is not described as certification.
- [ ] Product limitations and untested areas are visible.

## Launch Assets

- [ ] Demo video recorded from the approved script.
- [ ] Product screenshots contain no secrets, personal data, or customer identifiers.
- [ ] LinkedIn launch post reviewed.
- [ ] Product Hunt title, tagline, description, maker comment, and FAQ reviewed.
- [ ] Founder story explains the problem and evidence without fearmongering.
- [ ] FAQ covers security scope, latency, storage, pricing, support, and authorization.

## Operations

- [ ] Owner assigned for support, incidents, demos, billing, and social replies.
- [ ] Dashboard event conventions are active.
- [ ] Daily launch-week review scheduled.
- [ ] Rollback and incident communication procedures confirmed.
- [ ] Capacity and rate limits reviewed.
