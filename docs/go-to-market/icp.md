# CyberRakshak Guard Ideal Customer Profiles

CyberRakshak Guard is an OWASP LLM Top 10 aligned AI security gateway that helps teams detect, block, redact, monitor, and report risks in chatbots, RAG applications, and AI agents. It supports defense-in-depth risk reduction; it does not guarantee complete protection.

## Segment Priority

1. AI chatbot agencies
2. RAG chatbot builders
3. Small SaaS companies with AI support
4. Enterprise pilot customers
5. WordPress/WooCommerce chatbot owners

Agency partners are the recommended first target because one relationship can produce multiple protected client deployments.

## Segment A: AI Chatbot Agencies

**Profile:** Agencies and freelancers building chatbots or AI automations for three or more clients.

**Pain points**

- Client security questions delay delivery.
- No repeatable protection, monitoring, or reporting layer.
- Difficult to offer ongoing value after chatbot implementation.
- Client data and logs must remain separated.

**Product value**

- Client dashboard, Guard API/SDK, white-label reports, badge, alerts, and tenant isolation.
- A recurring AI security add-on the agency can operate.

**Best pitch**

You build AI chatbots. CyberRakshak Guard lets you sell secure AI chatbot protection as a monthly add-on.

**Pricing range to validate:** INR 9,999/month platform plan, partner pricing for active client volume, and an agency-managed service fee set by the partner.

**Sales channels:** LinkedIn founder outreach, WhatsApp freelancer groups, agency communities, referrals, demo workshops.

**Expected objections**

- Clients will not pay for security.
- The model provider already includes safety controls.
- Support effort may reduce agency margin.
- White-label reports must fit the agency brand.

**Conversion strategy:** Free internal demo workspace, first three client projects free for 14 days, first report delivered with the agency, then convert the partner based on active clients.

## Segment B: RAG Chatbot Builders

**Profile:** Teams building PDF, policy, knowledge-base, or private-document assistants.

**Pain points**

- Prompt injection embedded in documents.
- Private document leakage and weak retrieval isolation.
- No-source answers and unsupported claims.
- Difficult review process before documents are indexed.

**Product value**

- Document sandboxing, OCR controls, quarantine/review, vector namespace isolation, ACL filtering, and grounding guard.

**Best pitch**

Add a defensive review and retrieval-control layer before private documents reach the model or the final answer reaches a user.

**Pricing range to validate:** INR 2,999-9,999/month based on request volume, project count, and RAG operations; custom for high-volume or self-hosted use.

**Sales channels:** RAG developer communities, AI consultancies, technical LinkedIn content, GitHub/demo referrals.

**Expected objections:** Latency, false positives, existing vector-database permissions, document privacy, and external-service dependence.

**Conversion strategy:** Authorized free audit using synthetic or approved documents, followed by a 14-day guarded RAG evaluation with explicit leakage and grounding success criteria.

## Segment C: Small SaaS Companies

**Profile:** 10-250 employee SaaS companies with a live or near-live customer support or product assistant.

**Pain points**

- Prompt injection and sensitive-data exposure.
- No searchable guard logs or compliance evidence.
- Enterprise prospects ask security questions the team cannot answer consistently.
- Small engineering team cannot build provider-independent guardrails.

**Product value**

- Input/output guards, policy, redaction, signed webhooks, reports, support, and trust documentation.

**Best pitch**

Ship customer-facing AI with reviewable security controls without building an entire AI security gateway internally.

**Pricing range to validate:** INR 999/month Starter and INR 2,999/month Pro, with annual prepayment options.

**Sales channels:** Founder-led LinkedIn/email, SaaS communities, security questionnaire referrals, accelerator networks.

**Expected objections:** Low current incident volume, competing priorities, integration time, and uncertainty about willingness to pay.

**Conversion strategy:** Guided beta focused on first guarded request within 48 hours, 100+ messages scanned, first report, and a day-14 paid decision.

## Segment D: WordPress/WooCommerce Chatbot Owners

**Profile:** SMB sites using a plugin or hosted chatbot for support and commerce.

**Pain points:** Limited technical resources, customer PII in chat, weak visibility, and need for a simple trust signal.

**Product value:** Simple guard integration, basic report, monitoring, and security badge.

**Best pitch:** Add a visible protection and reporting layer to your store chatbot without running a security team.

**Pricing range to validate:** INR 499-999/month for a future plugin-led package; do not build the plugin until demand is proven.

**Sales channels:** WordPress agencies, plugin communities, hosting partners, WooCommerce consultants.

**Expected objections:** Setup complexity, price sensitivity, and reliance on the chatbot vendor.

**Conversion strategy:** Treat as a later channel experiment. First validate at least 10 qualified requests and partner distribution before product investment.

## Segment E: Enterprise Pilot Customers

**Profile:** Regulated or high-scale organizations requiring SSO, SCIM, SIEM, audit logs, self-hosting, retention, or formal operating controls.

**Pain points:** Security approval, private-data exposure, fragmented AI controls, audit evidence, identity integration, and operational ownership.

**Product value:** Enterprise controls plus a scoped integration and authorized validation package.

**Best pitch:** Validate one owned AI workflow with measurable risk-reduction, latency, detection-quality, and operations criteria.

**Pricing range to validate:** Free limited pilot for tightly qualified strategic accounts; paid pilot INR 25,000-1,00,000 depending on scope; annual pricing custom.

**Sales channels:** Founder network, security leaders, enterprise referrals, agencies, accelerators, and targeted account outreach.

**Expected objections:** Vendor maturity, SLA, deployment model, procurement, false-positive risk, and proof of production reliability.

**Conversion strategy:** Time-boxed 14- or 30-day pilot with named owners, signed success criteria, final recommendation, and a scheduled commercial decision.

## Qualification Questions

1. Which owned AI workflow is live or launching within 60 days?
2. What sensitive data can enter prompts, documents, tools, or outputs?
3. What current control detects and records unsafe behavior?
4. What customer, security, or compliance event created urgency?
5. Who owns integration, security approval, and budget?
6. What must a beta or pilot prove for the buyer to continue?
7. What would stop the purchase?

## Disqualifiers

- Wants an absolute security guarantee or certification claim.
- Wants testing against an unauthorized chatbot or third-party system.
- Has no use case, owner, timeline, or decision process.
- Requires raw secrets or unredacted customer data in sales/support workflows.
- Requests unrelated features before validating the core security outcome.
