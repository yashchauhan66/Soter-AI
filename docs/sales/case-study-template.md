# Case Study Creation Workflow

## Permission Gate

Do not publish the customer name, logo, quote, screenshots, architecture, metrics, or findings without written approval. Anonymous publication also requires approval because context may identify the customer.

## Workflow

1. Confirm beta/pilot success and customer willingness.
2. Agree on public versus confidential information.
3. Draft from redacted evidence and approved metrics.
4. Send exact copy, screenshots, quote, and logo usage for approval.
5. Record approver, date, channels, and expiry/revocation terms.
6. Publish only the approved version.
7. Review after major product/customer changes.

## Structure

### 1. Customer Type

Industry, company stage, and AI workflow. Use a generic description unless naming is approved.

### 2. Problem

What operational or security problem created urgency?

### 3. Chatbot Risk

Which prompt injection, data leakage, unsafe output, RAG, or monitoring risks were in scope?

### 4. Integration Time

Record setup time, owners, and any customer-side dependencies.

### 5. What CyberRakshak Guard Detected

Use counts/categories and redacted examples only.

### 6. What Was Blocked or Redacted

Explain expected action, policy, and remaining limitations.

### 7. Report Outcome

Summarize evidence generated and how the team used it.

### 8. Business Value

Examples: reduced custom engineering, better enterprise security response, faster review, or a new agency service. Do not invent ROI.

### 9. Quote/Testimonial

Use only customer-approved wording with attribution scope.

### 10. Before/After

| Before | After |
| --- | --- |
| Existing control and evidence gap | Measured workflow after integration |

## Required Limitations

- Results cover only the approved workflow and period.
- Detection and policy require ongoing review.
- CyberRakshak Guard provides defense-in-depth risk reduction, not complete protection.
