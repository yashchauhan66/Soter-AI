# Pricing Validation Plan

## Objective

Validate packaging, willingness to pay, and buying friction before changing product scope or publishing unsupported price claims.

## Current Hypothesis

| Plan | Current hypothesis | Primary buyer | Value metric |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Free | INR 0 | Developer evaluator | One project and limited monthly requests |
| Starter | INR 999/month | Solo founder or small SaaS | Production chatbot operations |
| Pro | INR 2,999/month | Growing SaaS team | Higher volume, projects, and evidence |
| Agency | INR 9,999/month | Digital agency | Client portfolio and white-label operations |
| Enterprise | Custom | Security/platform leadership | Scale, controls, deployment, and support |

These are hypotheses, not validated market prices.

## Pricing Questions

1. Is request volume the clearest value metric, or should projects/workspaces lead?
2. Does the buyer value prevention, evidence, compliance response, or reduced engineering effort most?
3. Is Agency priced as software only or bundled with partner enablement?
4. Will enterprises pay for a pilot, and should pilot fees be credited to annual contracts?
5. Which controls create a credible Enterprise boundary without artificially crippling lower plans?

## Research Method

### Buyer Interviews

Run at least:

- 10 qualified SaaS interviews.
- 8 agency interviews.
- 6 enterprise buyer interviews.

Ask after problem discovery, not at the start:

- What would you do without this product?
- What does the current workaround cost in engineering or risk?
- Which package would you choose and why?
- At what monthly price does this feel too cheap to trust?
- At what price is it a good value?
- At what price does it become expensive but still considered?
- At what price is it too expensive?

### Offer Tests

Test one variable at a time:

1. 14-day trial versus guided beta.
2. Monthly versus annual with two months equivalent discount.
3. Usage-led versus project-led packaging.
4. Paid enterprise pilot versus free proof of concept.
5. Agency platform fee versus per-client fee.

Do not run deceptive pricing pages. Quotes must state what is included and whether pricing is experimental.

## Validation Thresholds

Starter/Pro signal:

- 40%+ of activated qualified beta accounts request continued access.
- 25%+ accept a paid plan without custom product work.
- Fewer than 20% cite confusing packaging as the primary blocker.

Agency signal:

- Three partners propose the service to a real client.
- Two partners deploy a paying client.
- Partner gross-margin expectation remains viable after support effort.

Enterprise signal:

- At least three qualified buyers accept paid-pilot framing.
- One paid pilot converts or reaches procurement with agreed success criteria.

## Discount Policy

- No permanent founder discounts without an expiry and reason.
- Annual discounts require prepayment.
- Pilot credits must be written into the proposal.
- Agency discounts depend on active client volume, not promises.
- Record list price, offered price, reason, approver, and renewal price.

## Interview Record

- Account and ICP segment.
- Current workaround and estimated cost.
- Trigger event and urgency.
- Selected package.
- Price reactions at each threshold.
- Procurement/security blockers.
- Decision and next date.
- Exact objections, excluding sensitive customer data.

## Decision Rules

- Change pricing only after a pattern appears across at least five qualified accounts.
- Change packaging when buyers repeatedly misunderstand value or plan boundaries.
- Add product scope only when the same blocker prevents multiple qualified buyers from paying.
- Preserve a clear free evaluation path without making it a substitute for production use.
